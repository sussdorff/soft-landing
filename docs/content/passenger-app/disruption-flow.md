# Disruption Flow

Step-by-step walkthrough of the passenger experience during a disruption.

## 1. Disruption Notification

When a disruption is detected, you receive a notification with:

- **What happened** — plain-language explanation (not airline jargon)
- **Why** — the root cause (weather, mechanical, operational)
- **What it means for you** — which of your flights is affected

!!! example "Example notification"
    "Heavy snowfall in Munich has caused flight LH 456 MUC → FCO to be cancelled. Your connecting flight from Berlin will arrive as scheduled, but you'll need a new connection to Rome."

## 2. Default Rebooking

The airline has already rebooked you on the next available option. The app shows:

- **Your default rebooking** — what the airline assigned automatically
- **Alternatives** — 2–3 other options that might work better for you

You can accept the default or choose something different.

## 3. View Your Options

Each option includes full details:

### Rebook (Alternative Flight)
- Flight number, route, departure and arrival times
- Estimated total travel time
- Connection details if applicable

### Hotel + Next-Day Flight
- Hotel name and location
- Complimentary overnight stay
- Next-day flight details
- Checkout time and transfer info

### Ground Transport
- Mode: train (ICE/EC) or bus
- Route and duration
- Departure time and station/stop
- Arrival at final destination

### Alternative Airport
- Flight to a nearby airport
- Ground transfer to your original destination
- Total estimated travel time

## 4. Submit Your Preference

Select your preferred option and submit it as a **wish**. You can rank multiple options if you want fallbacks.

!!! info "Your preference is a hint"
    Submitting a wish doesn't guarantee that option. The gate agent reviews all wishes considering seat availability, conflicts, and priority. Most wishes are approved, but some may need adjustment.

## 5. Wait for Approval

After submitting, your status shows as **pending**. The gate agent reviews wishes in priority order:

- First-time wishes are processed in submission order
- If you've been denied before, you get higher priority

The app shows real-time status — no need to refresh.

## 6. Resolution

### Approved

Your wish is confirmed. The app shows:

- Confirmed option details
- Updated itinerary
- Any next steps (go to gate, check in to hotel, find train platform)

### Denied

If the gate agent can't fulfill your preference, you'll see:

- The reason for denial
- Your new priority level (bumped up)
- Updated options to choose from

!!! tip "Priority escalation"
    After each denial, your priority increases automatically:

    - **1 denial** — you rank above passengers making their first choice
    - **2 denials** — you get the highest priority level

    This ensures nobody gets stuck in an endless loop of denials.

## 7. Resubmit (If Denied)

If denied, review the updated options and submit a new preference. With your elevated priority, the gate agent will see your wish near the top of the queue.

---

## Status Summary

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Notified** | You've been informed of the disruption |
| **Chose** | You've submitted a preference |
| **Approved** | The gate agent confirmed your option |
| **Denied** | Your preference was denied — pick a new option |
